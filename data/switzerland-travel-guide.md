## Summary
Switzerland offers a mix of dramatic mountain scenery, clear lakes, adventure activities, historic cities, and traditional food. Based on a 10-day trip, the article highlights experiences in Interlaken, Zermatt, and Zurich, along with several day trips and shorter visits to other Swiss cities.

## Things To Do
- Swim or spend a relaxing day on Lake Thun near Interlaken.
- Enjoy the scenery around Lake Brienz and walk the lakeside route from Interlaken.
- Go tandem paragliding above Interlaken for aerial views of the surrounding mountains and lakes.
- Ride the funicular to the Harder Kulm viewpoint overlooking Interlaken and its two lakes.
- Take a day trip from Interlaken to the high-altitude Jungfraujoch attraction.
- Hike a section of the exposed Hardergrat ridge between Brienz and Oberried am Brienzersee.
- Follow easier walking trails along Interlaken's river and Lake Brienz.
- Visit Lauterbrunnen on a short train trip from Interlaken.
- Ride the steep Gelmerbahn funicular and explore the lake at the top.
- Walk and cycle through the car-free streets of Zermatt.
- See the Matterhorn, the distinctive mountain associated with the Toblerone logo.
- Paraglide above Zermatt, including routes departing from Riffelberg.
- Hike the Five Lakes route near Zermatt for mountain and lake scenery.
- Ride the Matterhorn Glacier Paradise cable car for elevated views.
- Take the Gornergrat cog railway and stop near Rotenboden to visit Riffelsee Lake.
- Swim in Lake Zurich when conditions are suitable.
- Walk along the shore of Lake Zurich.
- Visit the Lindt chocolate museum to learn about chocolate production and sample chocolate.
- Try traditional Swiss cheese fondue and raclette in Zurich.
- Take a day trip from Zurich to Liechtenstein.
- Travel from Zurich to ride the Sattel-Hochstuckli alpine slide.
- Explore Basel on a self-guided walking tour.
- Discover Lucerne on foot and take in its history and architecture.
- Walk around Bern on a self-guided city tour.
- Enjoy mountain scenery while travelling through Switzerland by train.

## Notes From The Article
- The trip lasted 10 days, with most of the time spent in Zurich, Interlaken, and Zermatt.
- Interlaken sits between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz and serves as a convenient base for outdoor activities and nearby excursions.
- Early September brought good weather for lake activities during the visit described.
- Paragliding was considered better value than paying for some expensive mountain viewpoints.
- Twin Paragliding Interlaken offered English-speaking pilots and complimentary pickup from nearby accommodation.
- Harder Kulm stands 1,322 metres above Interlaken and mainly offers a viewpoint and restaurant at the top.
- The Harder Kulm trip was described as costing around $37 USD per person.
- Jungfraujoch cost more than $100 USD per person at the time of the trip.
- The Hardergrat Trail is exposed, with steep drops on both sides, making it suitable for experienced and confident hikers.
- Lauterbrunnen is about 30 minutes from Interlaken by train.
- The Gelmerbahn has a 106-degree incline and travels at 4.5 miles per hour.
- Reaching the Gelmerbahn is easiest by car, though a bus service also runs from Interlaken.
- Zermatt is largely car-free, with exceptions including doctors, police, and taxis.
- The Matterhorn is described as a pyramid-shaped mountain reaching nearly 5,000 metres.
- The travellers felt they should have allocated more than a couple of days to Zermatt.
- Fly Zermatt operates several paragliding options, including the Scenic flight from Riffelberg and a higher Elite experience.
- Poor weather prevented the travellers from completing their planned Five Lakes hike.
- The Gornergrat cog railway was included in the travellers' Eurail pass.
- Riffelsee Lake, close to Rotenboden railway station, was highlighted as a strong location for viewing the Matterhorn.
- Swiss Chuchi Restaurant in Zurich dates to 1953 and was described as the first fondue parlour in the city's old town.
- Swiss Chuchi is located inside Hotel Adler Zurich, a building with a history extending back 500 years.
- Train-station luggage storage made it easier to explore Basel, Lucerne, and Bern during shorter stops.
- Geneva and Lausanne were mentioned as destinations the travellers hoped to visit on a future trip.
