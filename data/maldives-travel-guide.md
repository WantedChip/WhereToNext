## Summary
The Maldives offers far more than private resorts, with local islands, marine wildlife, snorkeling, diving, cultural experiences, and tropical beaches spread across a country of roughly 1,200 islands. Based on a 20-day trip across six islands, the article highlights both luxury stays and more affordable local-island adventures.

## Things To Do
- Scuba dive or snorkel with whale sharks and manta rays in South Ari Atoll.
- Stay at a boutique hotel on a local island for a more affordable luxury-style experience.
- Swim with large groups of nurse sharks near Fulidhoo in Vaavu Atoll.
- Tour Malé to learn about Maldivian history, culture, markets, and everyday life.
- Take a boat trip to a remote sandbank surrounded by clear shallow water.
- Spend a night in an overwater bungalow with direct access to the sea.
- Try traditional handline fishing and cook the catch on an uninhabited island.
- Join a beach cleanup and learn about conservation efforts on a local island.
- Snorkel house reefs and coral gardens to see fish, turtles, stingrays, sharks, and other marine life.
- Visit Mudhdhoo, also known as Vaadhoo Island, to look for glowing bioluminescent plankton at night.

## Notes From The Article
- The authors spent 20 days exploring six islands during their visit.
- The Maldives is made up of about 1,200 islands, with fewer than 200 inhabited.
- Local islands can provide access to beaches and excursions similar to resort islands at a much lower cost.
- South Ari Atoll was highlighted as a place where whale sharks and manta rays can be seen throughout the year.
- Whale sharks and manta rays often feed near the surface, making snorkeling a suitable alternative for non-divers.
- The authors spent five days diving in South Ari Atoll and stayed at a local guesthouse.
- Fulhadhoo has a population of about 400 people and only a small number of guesthouses, which meant the beach was often quiet.
- Activities on Fulhadhoo included snorkeling, handline fishing, an island tour, karaoke, and a private beachfront dinner.
- The nurse shark excursion took place a short boat ride from Fulidhoo, where more than 100 sharks may gather.
- The nurse sharks in the area can exceed 10 feet in length, and the excursion was conducted with guides familiar with their behavior.
- Malé is home to roughly one quarter of the country's population despite the Maldives having nearly 200 inhabited islands.
- Highlights of the capital tour included the fish market and an elevated city view from a rooftop restaurant.
- Malé is connected by bridge to the airport island of Hulhumalé, making it a practical early stop after arrival.
- The sandbank visit followed the nurse shark excursion and included about 45 minutes of relaxing, wading, and taking photos.
- The Maldives has more than 100 resort islands, many offering overwater accommodation.
- Some resort islands require seaplane transfers that may cost an additional $400–$500 per person.
- Food and all-inclusive packages at private resorts can add significantly to the total cost.
- The authors used credit card points for a two-night overwater bungalow stay.
- The handline fishing trip used octopus as bait before the guides grilled the catch on a nearby uninhabited island.
- The beach cleanup on Guraidhoo combined volunteer work with a local island tour and discussion about pollution and conservation.
- The cleanup experience also included hedhikaa, a local afternoon tea tradition.
- The article recommends adding time on a local island even when the main trip is centered on a private resort.
- House reefs near the visited islands offered easy access to fish and coral without requiring scuba diving.
- Snorkelers may encounter turtles, stingrays, nurse sharks, manta rays, and whale sharks.
- The authors did not personally visit the bioluminescent beach on Mudhdhoo Island but included it as a future travel goal.
- The article states that the reported peak period for bioluminescence is from June through October, when the water is warmer.
