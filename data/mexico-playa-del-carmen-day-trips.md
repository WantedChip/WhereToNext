## Summary
This guide uses Playa del Carmen as a base for exploring the Riviera Maya and the wider Yucatan Peninsula. It organizes nearby ruins, islands, cenotes, wildlife encounters, lagoons, adventure parks, and towns into day-trip ideas that can be reached from the city.

## Things To Do
- Visit Chichen Itza early to see El Castillo, the ball court, temple ruins, and sacred cenote before the busiest crowds arrive.
- Explore Coba by bike or bicycle taxi so there is enough time to reach the climbable pyramid and scattered ruins.
- Add Ek Balam for another Yucatan ruins stop with structures visitors can still climb.
- Go skydiving from downtown Playa del Carmen and land near the beach.
- Take the ferry to Isla Mujeres for beaches, golf-cart exploring, Punta Sur, and seasonal whale shark tours.
- Spend a night or long day on Isla Holbox for sandbars, ocean hammocks, low-key island streets, and lobster pizza.
- Ride the ferry to Cozumel for beaches, Punta Sur Ecological Park, El Cielo, moped exploring, and scuba diving.
- Tour Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve and float through the ancient canal surrounded by mangroves and wildlife.
- Plan a lagoon day in Bacalar if bright blue water and a slower pace are the priority.
- Dive Dos Ojos or another cenote to see clear water, caverns, stalactites, and rock formations.
- Combine Cenote Maya with rappelling, ziplines, a Mayan ceremony, and a local meal.
- Choose Emotions Native Park for a compact ATV, zipline, cenote, and jungle activity day near Playa.
- Visit Tulum for beach access, ruins, cenotes, restaurants, and photo stops along the beach road.
- Snorkel at Akumal with a guide or from the permitted beach areas to look for sea turtles.
- Walk Valladolid's central square, cathedral, market, and colorful streets as an inland Yucatan stop.

## Notes From The Article
- Chichen Itza is described as crowded, so the article recommends arriving when the gates open.
- Cozumel is the easiest island day trip from Playa del Carmen because the ferry leaves from downtown.
- Holbox is possible as a day trip but works better with one or two nights because of the drive and ferry connection.
- Some Akumal turtle-swim rules had changed, so current access limits and guide requirements should be checked before going.
