## Summary
Argentina combines major cities, subtropical waterfalls, Patagonian glaciers, mountain landscapes, and distinctive cultural experiences within one country. The article focuses on highlights from Iguazú Falls, Buenos Aires, and Argentine Patagonia, including boat tours, tango, dining, glacier kayaking, and a demanding hike to Fitz Roy.

## Things To Do
- Explore the Argentine side of Iguazú Falls using its jungle trails and the 1,100-meter catwalk above the waterfalls.
- Take a boat tour at Iguazú Falls for close views and a ride beneath some of the smaller cascades.
- Stay inside the national park for direct views of Iguazú Falls and earlier access to the surrounding trails.
- Visit Buenos Aires landmarks and neighborhoods including La Recoleta Cemetery, Palermo, Casa Rosada, and La Boca.
- Browse El Ateneo Grand Splendid, a former grand theater converted into a bookstore.
- Attend a Buenos Aires tango experience with dinner and a live performance in a century-old theater.
- Join a traditional multi-course asado meal and dine with fellow travelers at a shared table.
- Kayak on the glacial lake in front of Perito Moreno Glacier.
- Walk the viewpoints and trails overlooking Perito Moreno's enormous wall of ice.
- Hike the Laguna de Los Tres trail to reach views at the base of the Fitz Roy peaks.
- Travel through Patagonia between El Calafate and the hiking town of El Chaltén.

## Notes From The Article
- Iguazú Falls is described as 1.5 times taller and almost three times wider than Niagara Falls.
- The waterfalls span the border between Argentina and Brazil, and the article recommends experiencing both sides because each offers a different perspective.
- The Brazilian side is highlighted for its view toward Devil's Throat, while the Argentine side is known for hiking trails and elevated walkways.
- The Iguazú boat tour is a soaking experience, but dry bags are provided for cameras and phones.
- The boat does not approach Devil's Throat because of safety concerns, though it offers distant views and passes beneath smaller waterfalls.
- Gran Meliá Iguazú is the only hotel located inside the Argentine national park and is at least 40 minutes from the nearest city.
- Staying inside the park gives guests an earlier start on the waterfall trails, and the authors spotted toucans and capuchin monkeys around the property.
- Buenos Aires is nicknamed the "Paris of the South" and was presented as a modern city with a strong bookstore culture.
- The Piazzolla tango experience included lessons, dinner, and a professional performance, although the authors missed the lesson portion.
- The asado dining experience consisted of five courses with wine pairings and was served at a communal, family-style table.
- Patagonia extends across both Argentina and Chile and covers a very large area with many outdoor attractions.
- Perito Moreno Glacier forms part of the Southern Patagonian Icefield and has an accessible ice wall rising about 240 feet.
- Visitors can experience Perito Moreno through trails, boat tours, ice trekking, or kayaking.
- The kayaking activity kept participants at a safe distance from the glacier because large pieces of ice can break away.
- Dry suits and kayak skirts were provided for the near-freezing water.
- The Laguna de Los Tres hike is approximately 12–13 miles round trip and typically takes 7–10 hours.
- Reaching El Chaltén from El Calafate requires about a 2.5-hour bus ride or drive.
- The article recommends booking early for front-row seats on the upper level of the bus to get better views during the journey.
- Much of the Laguna de Los Tres trail is relatively flat, but the beginning and final section are more difficult.
- The last kilometer of the hike is especially strenuous, with rocky switchbacks climbing the mountainside.
- Starting the Laguna de Los Tres hike early is recommended.
- The Fitz Roy hike was compared with the Base Towers hike in Chile's Torres del Paine as one of Patagonia's best-known day hikes.
- Other Argentine destinations the authors wanted to visit but did not reach included Ushuaia, Mendoza, Bariloche, Córdoba, Salta, and Tigre.
- The article suggests booking intercity transport in advance, with bus routes available to destinations including Buenos Aires, El Chaltén, and El Calafate.
